# BarBell Landing Page — Build Prompt for Code

## Project Context

BarBell is a B2B SaaS product sold to gym operators. It is NOT a consumer fitness app. The landing page is a sales tool targeting independent and boutique gym owners who want to grow their brand, reduce member churn, and unlock new revenue. The page must feel like it was built specifically for serious gym operators — not like a generic SaaS startup site.

---

## Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark base with clean, light elements. Think premium gym energy — heavy iron, controlled lighting, serious atmosphere. Not a tech startup. Not corporate SaaS. Sophisticated, industry-native, and refined.

**Tone:** Confident. Direct. No fluff. This page speaks to operators who built something with their hands and take their brand seriously.

**Typography:**
- Wordmark: "BARBELL" in a bold, industrial serif or strong geometric sans — something that commands attention and reads like a brand, not a product name. The wordmark carries the entire visual identity.
- Headlines: Same family as the wordmark or a complementary bold display font. Heavy weight. Tight tracking.
- Body: Clean, legible, refined. Something with character — not Inter, not Roboto, not system fonts. Consider something like DM Serif Display, Playfair Display, Bebas Neue for display, paired with a clean humanist for body.
- Import from Google Fonts.

**Color Palette:**
- Background: Deep charcoal or near-black (not pure #000000 — something like #0D0D0D, #111111, or #0F0F0F)
- Primary accent: A single strong accent color — consider warm white (#F5F0E8), or a bold single-tone like a deep red (#C0392B) or muted gold (#C9A84C) for highlights and CTAs. Pick one and commit.
- Text: Off-white (#F0EDE6 or similar) for primary, muted gray for secondary
- Form and card surfaces: Slightly lighter dark (#1A1A1A or #1C1C1C) with subtle borders
- No gradients except where intentional and restrained. No purple gradients. No generic glow effects.

**Motion:**
- Staggered fade-in on scroll for sections
- Subtle reveal animations on stat numbers (count up on scroll into view)
- Form fields have clean focus states
- CTA button has a confident hover state — no bouncing, no over-animation
- One strong hero animation — text reveal or a clean fade-in sequence on load

**Layout:**
- Single page, vertical scroll
- Full-width sections with generous vertical padding
- Asymmetric where it serves the content — not forced
- Hero takes full viewport height
- No sidebar. No tabs. No carousels.
- Mobile responsive — this page will be opened on a phone by gym owners checking email

### Section 4 — How BarBell Works (The Mechanism)

**The job of this section:** Show how BarBell delivers retention through relationship — make it feel human, not robotic.

**Section label:** HOW IT WORKS

**Headline:**
> Your members need someone in their corner. BarBell is that person.

**Four feature blocks (icon + headline + 2-3 lines each):**

1. **It checks in between visits**
   BarBell detects when a member is near your gym and sends a personalized nudge — factoring in real-time gym traffic. Not a generic push notification. A message that feels like it came from someone who knows them.
   *"Hey Marcus — the gym is quieter than usual right now. Good time to get that session in."*

2. **It knows your gym, not a generic template**
   Every workout BarBell generates is built around your specific equipment. A member at your gym gets programming for your floor — not a Planet Fitness template, not a CrossFit box template. Yours.

3. **It gets smarter with every session**
   BarBell tracks performance over time and adapts programming automatically — adjusting volume, intensity, and exercise selection as members progress. A coach that never forgets a session. Never has an off day. Scales to every member simultaneously.

4. **It makes your brand the constant in their life**
   Your members associate their progress with your gym — not an app they downloaded themselves. BarBell runs under your brand. Your name. Your identity. You become the thing that helped them change.

## Technical Requirements

- **Stack:** Single HTML file — HTML, CSS, JavaScript all in one file. No external frameworks. No React. No build tools required.
- **Fonts:** Google Fonts via @import or link tag
- **Icons:** Use simple SVG icons inline or a CDN icon set (Tabler Icons via CDN is fine) — no image dependencies
- **Animations:** CSS animations and vanilla JS scroll observers (IntersectionObserver) — no GSAP required unless the developer prefers it
- **Stat counter:** Numbers in Section 3 count up when scrolled into view — vanilla JS
- **Form:** Fully functional frontend validation. On submit, hide form and show confirmation state. POST endpoint is a placeholder — developer wires backend.
- **Calendly:** Placeholder link `YOUR_CALENDLY_URL` in the confirmation state — developer swaps in real URL
- **Mobile responsive:** Full mobile breakpoints. Navigation collapses cleanly. Form is full width on mobile. Stat cards stack vertically.
- **No stock images required** — design should work entirely with typography, color, and geometric/typographic visual elements
- **Performance:** No unnecessary dependencies. Page should load fast.
